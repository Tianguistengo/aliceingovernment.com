Frequently Asked Questions

Climate Change is too important for us to rely on Governments to fix it. It is us, the citizens, that should decide how to respond to it. The goal of this platform is for you, me, and people from all over the world, to vote on how we think we should we react to Climate Change.

Your vote, like everyone else’s, will create a citizen consensus that can bring about all sorts of changes such as financing top solutions, adjusting public budgets, creating new projects, pressuring Governments, adjusting international agreements, and whatever our collective imagination comes up with. Phase 2 of this project will be all about choosing and putting those potential applications on track but for now, go explore our voters section to read about how other people think we should react to climate change, invite people that would enjoy sharing their opinion, and follow our updates on Twitter.

Despite there being more than one scientific study about how to respond to climate change effectively (visit Planetary Boundaries for an example), we chose Drawdown due to its 200+ team of researchers and academics, their robust legitimacy among the scientific community, and its inclusion of economic factors and feasibility studies.
